Metric guide

These short definitions keep interpretation consistent across collaborators when reviewing spread, uncertainty, and data-quality indicators.

Coefficient of variation Coefficient of variation expresses spread relative to the mean, which is useful when comparing stability across metrics with different scales.
95% confidence interval The 95% confidence interval gives the plausible range for the cohort mean, assuming the filtered rows represent the underlying population reasonably well.
Potential outliers Outliers are flagged with the interquartile-range rule and should be checked against raw rows, sample notes, or instrument-specific measurement issues.
Missing values High missingness reduces confidence in downstream summaries and can bias group comparisons if missing records are not random.
